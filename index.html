Making Ideas

Singapore Space Challenge 2021

My team and I participated in the Singapore Space Challenge 2021. The mission was to design a rover capable of carrying out excavation and/or In-Situ Utilisation on the Moon. Our project won the Women in STEM award!

We designed a rover with two parts: the "Worm" and the Moon Ground Station, which are connected by cable. The worm would dig into the lunar regolith to extract ice, while the ground station would supply power to it from above.

The worm is also capable of performing manouvers to traverse tricky terrain in craters.

International Space Challenge 2022

The International Space Challenge 2022's Starter Category mission was to design an experiment on the International Space Station to study the Sun and its effects. Our project was about the effects of solar radiation on the mutation of E-coli bacteria in space.

Our team won the Best Poster Award, consisting of a $500 cash prize!
